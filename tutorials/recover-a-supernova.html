Recover a supernova in Kepler data¶

This tutorial demonstrates how to recover a known supernova signal from the Kepler prime mission data.

The Kepler prime mission observed about 500 galaxies over several years, discovering about a dozen supernovae and other extragalactic transients. K2 continues extragalactic science, having observed over 40,000 galaxies. Extragalactic science with K2 falls into two categories: transient searches and active galactic nuclei timescales. In this tutorial we focus on the former.

KSN 2011d¶

Garnavich et al. 2016 identified a Type IIp supernova KSN2011d in the host galaxy KIC 10649106. First let’s look at the raw supernova lightcurve.
